Evidence: iter 10 (14 trials, Brier 0.14377), iter 13 (14 trials, 0.143759), iter 15 (26 trials, hpo_objective 0.088224) — all DISCARDs due to trial starvation, not because the ranges are wrong. The critical proof is iter 15 vs iter 14: narrowed ranges reached hpo_objective 0.088224 vs wide-range best of 0.088223 (delta = 0.000001, pure noise). The narrowed optimum is mechanically equivalent. Now that iter 18 confirms 30 trials complete at train_bars=10000 with wide ranges, the per-trial cost is known. Narrowed ranges reduce per-trial variance, so each trial converges faster — 55-cap should yield 38-42 trials. Target ranges: n_estimators [500, 1500], learning_rate [0.005, 0.04], max_depth [3, 6], num_leaves [31, 96], min_child_samples [200, 600]. Keep reg_alpha, reg_lambda, subsample, colsample_bytree at current wide bounds (no convergence data to narrow). Do NOT change any other knob simultaneously. Expected gain: Brier reduction 0.0005-0.002 from better search convergence within the confirmed-optimal region; bs_sharpe target >93.

2. **Add 4th time_pct at 0.10 — set time_pcts to [0.10, 0.30, 0.50, 0.80].** Gate: only attempt after priority #1 confirms >35 trials completing (which validates that per-trial cost is manageable at this sample size). The 0.10 point captures first-10%-of-bar momentum (~30s on a 5m bar), a qualitatively different intra-bar phase from the current [0.30, 0.50, 0.80] set. The previous 4-point failure (iter 12, Brier 0.16192) used densely-clustered mid-bar points [0.30, 0.40, 0.60, 0.80] and was confounded by HPO starvation (17 trials). The proposed set is geometrically diverse and anchored on the confirmed 3-point optimum. Sample count rises ~33% (185K → 247K), so trial completions will drop proportionally. Set trial cap to 60 for this experiment. If fewer than 25 trials complete, abort and flag as budget-constrained. Expected gain: Brier reduction 0.002-0.005 if early-bar signal is informative.

3. **Test purge_period 12→24 bars.** Not yet tested at the current baseline (iter 8 forward). The iter 9 blacklist applies to embargo_period only — purge_period is a distinct and untested parameter. Regime features (regime_vol_zscore, vol_window=120) have the slowest-decaying autocorrelation of any feature in the set; 12 bars (1 hour on 5m) may not fully clear this feature's autocorrelation. A 24-bar purge (2 hours) removes the leakage risk from all features with window <= 120 bars. Effect is likely small on Brier but improves OOS reliability and PBO. Cost: 12 lost training bars per fold (0.12% of 10000 train_bars — negligible). Keep all other walk-forward params fixed (n_splits=8, train_bars=10000, test_bars=2000, embargo_period=6). This is a validation hygiene improvement with low downside.

4. **Test min_target_corr tightening 0.005→0.010 (auto-feature selection).** Run only after priorities #1 and #2 are resolved, so the comparison is against a fully HPO-converged baseline. The current threshold retains features with >0.5% target correlation. Tightening to 1.0% will auto-prune the weakest technical indicators per fold, reducing collinearity pressure on regime and liquidation features. Unlike iter 16 (static manual OI removal, DISCARD), adaptive per-fold pruning adjusts to regime changes rather than applying a fixed mask — this is a different mechanism and is not blacklisted. The protected_prefixes list ensures regime_, liquidation_, oi_, and funding_ features are never auto-pruned even if their correlation dips below threshold in a given fold. Expected effect: Brier reduction 0.001-0.003 from reduced collinearity noise; potentially fewer features → faster per-trial cost.

5. **Test n_splits 8→10 after per-trial cost is confirmed stable.** Previous failure (iter 11, 13 trials, DISCARD) occurred when the model had 4 time_pcts and high per-trial cost. Current model has 3 time_pcts and known-stable trial budget (30 trials at iter 18). Increasing n_splits from 8 to 10 adds 25% more walk-forward folds, improving OOS estimate stability and reducing the variance of the Brier estimate. Gate: confirm that priority #1 achieves >35 trial completions (validating budget headroom). If trial completions are marginal at the current config, defer this. Expected effect: lower OOS Brier variance (statistical hygiene), marginal Brier improvement (more diverse test windows), no significant PnL impact.
