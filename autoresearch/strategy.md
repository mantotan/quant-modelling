## Priority Queue

1. **Increase train_bars 8000→10000 — more historical context per fold.** The gate condition from the last directive is now satisfied: iter 14 confirmed 34 trials complete at 3 time_pcts with train_bars=8000 (well within budget). iter 8 produced a 27.5% Brier improvement when train_bars increased from 5000→8000, establishing a strong learning-curve gradient. The gradient may not be exhausted: 8000 bars spans 27.8 days on 5m; 10000 bars spans 34.7 days, covering more monthly seasonality. Per-trial cost increase is proportional (~25%), so at 34 trials budgeted, expect ~27 trials at 10000 bars — still above the minimum convergence threshold of 25. If Brier improves by >0.001 (1%), KEEP and re-verify. If gain is marginal (<0.0002), treat as saturated and move to priority #2. Do NOT simultaneously change HPO ranges — isolate the train_bars variable.

2. **Apply narrowed HPO ranges after train_bars experiment completes.** Convergence evidence: iter 10 (14 trials, narrowed ranges) achieved bs_sharpe 87.79 — the record at that model version. iter 15 (26 trials, narrowed ranges, post-iter-14 baseline) achieved hpo_objective 0.088224 vs best 0.088223 — essentially identical convergence, confirming the narrowed ranges reach the same optimum. The remaining obstacle is the trial cap: 26/40 trials completed at narrowed ranges (iter 15) vs 34/40 at wide ranges (iter 14). Resolution: increase fast_mode trial cap from 40 to 50 when applying narrowed ranges. The narrowed search space makes each trial faster (less diverse params to evaluate), so 50 trials at narrowed ranges should complete in roughly the same wall time as 40 trials at wide ranges. Target ranges: n_estimators [500,1500], learning_rate [0.005,0.04], max_depth [3,6], num_leaves [31,96], min_child_samples [200,600]. Expected gain: Brier reduction of 0.0005-0.002 from better HPO convergence; bs_sharpe target >92.

3. **Test a 4th time_pct at 0.10 (early-bar entry signal) — add [0.10, 0.30, 0.50, 0.80].** The current 3-point set captures mid-to-late bar dynamics. The 0.10 point (first 10% of bar, ~30s on 5m) provides early momentum signal before most participants react to bar open. iter 14 showed that reducing from 4 to 3 time_pcts improved Brier (iter 12 vs iter 14 was confounded by HPO starvation, but the clean result was +29.1% improvement). However, the iter-12 4-point set was [0.30, 0.40, 0.60, 0.80] — four densely-clustered mid-bar points. The [0.10, 0.30, 0.50, 0.80] set is geometrically diverse and captures a qualitatively different intra-bar phase. Sample count increases from ~185K to ~247K (+33%), pushing per-trial cost up. Gate: only attempt if train_bars experiment (priority #1) confirms budget headroom with >30 trials completing. If HPO runs <25 trials, abort and revert to 3 points.

4. **Test purge_period increase 12→24.** The current purge_period=12 bars (1 hour on 5m) prevents overlap between train and test windows. Intra-bar features (RSI-14, vol_10) have autocorrelation horizons of 14-70 bars; a 12-bar purge may not fully eliminate leakage from slow-moving features. Increasing to 24 bars (2 hours) more fully clears the autocorrelation horizon of the slowest-decaying feature (regime_vol_zscore with vol_window=120). The embargo_period blacklist (iter 9 DISCARD) applies to embargo only — purge is distinct and has not been tested at the current baseline. Expected effect: slightly lower apparent OOS Brier (reduced train-test leakage), potentially at the cost of 12 bars of lost training context per fold. This is a validation hygiene improvement, not a signal improvement.

5. **Test feature selection tightening — increase min_target_corr from 0.005 to 0.010.** The current threshold retains any feature with >0.5% target correlation. Tightening to 1.0% auto-prunes the weakest features before HPO. After pruning, OI features (oi_price_divergence, oi_momentum) and weaker technical indicators may be dropped, reducing collinearity pressure. iter 16 tested manual OI pruning and got DISCARD (0.101896 vs 0.101837) — but auto-pruning via corr threshold is different: it selects the pruned set dynamically per fold, adapting to regime changes rather than applying a static mask. Gate: run only after priorities #1 and #2 are complete, to have a clean HPO-converged baseline to compare against.
